Transfer Learning for Turbulence Generalization

Python TensorFlow Fluid Dynamics

This research project focused on enhancing the predictive accuracy of Sub-Grid Scale (SGS) models in turbulence simulations. By implementing transfer learning techniques, the goal was to improve the model's ability to generalize across different turbulent flow conditions without requiring complete retraining.

  • Investigated various pre-trained models and fine-tuning strategies to identify the most effective approach for SGS modeling.
  • Validated the improved model against high-fidelity Direct Numerical Simulation (DNS) data.
  • The results demonstrated a significant improvement in generalization, allowing for more efficient and accurate Large Eddy Simulations (LES).

Reacting Flow Solver

C++ Python Git Combustion

Developed a high-performance reacting flow solver from scratch in C++ to simulate complex combustion phenomena. The solver is capable of handling both 0D (homogeneous reactor) and 1D (propagating flame) simulations, providing a versatile tool for combustion research.

  • Implemented a detailed chemical kinetics mechanism involving 9 species and 21 elementary reactions.
  • Utilized efficient numerical methods for solving stiff ordinary differential equations (ODEs) inherent in combustion chemistry.
  • The tool is used to study ignition delay, flame speed, and species concentration profiles in various fuel-air mixtures.

GPU-based DNS N-S Solver

Python PyTorch Git HPC

This project involved the development of a 2D pseudo-spectral solver for Direct Numerical Simulation (DNS) of the Navier-Stokes equations. The primary goal was to simulate the decay of two-dimensional turbulence with high fidelity. A key feature is its implementation in Python with PyTorch, allowing for massive parallelization on GPUs.

  • Leveraged the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) for efficient computation of spatial derivatives in spectral space.
  • Achieved significant speed-up factors compared to traditional CPU-based solvers by offloading computations to the GPU.
  • A CPU-compatible fork was also created to enable simulations on HPC clusters that may not have GPU access.

Two-Phase Flow Heat Transfer Analysis

Ansys Fluent Matlab CFD

As part of my undergraduate thesis, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of heat transfer characteristics in two-phase (liquid-gas) fluid flow within complex geometries. This computational fluid dynamics (CFD) study aimed to understand and quantify heat exchange under various flow regimes.

  • Utilized the Drift-Flux model in Ansys Fluent, a computationally efficient approach for simulating two-phase flows where phases move at different velocities.
  • Performed parametric studies to analyze the effects of variables like inlet velocity, volume fraction, and heat flux on the overall heat transfer coefficient.
  • Post-processed and visualized the simulation data using MATLAB to extract meaningful physical insights.
